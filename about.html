My name's Tiffany, and I'm a storyteller.

I strive to write in ways that face the world by challenging it, often in metaphor. Lately, I've been learning and thinking a lot about social documentary photography, too.

My passions came at me through different angles: classes in visual anthropology, poetry, video art, life writing, sociolinguistics, French and Latin American storytelling, with themes from visual culture to the politics of memory and more. But also through online syllabi floating around the internet, and open-source audiovisual collectives, and women photographer communities, and the privilege of spare time for artistic experiementation.

Since I keep asking questions about the world, I'm a research strategist by trade. I'm also a volunteer research assistant for FemLENS, a nonprofit working with local community centres, libraries, activist groups and NGOs to provide documentary photography workshops to women and girls from diverse cultural and economic backgrounds. In my spare time, I enjoy spending time with friends and family, running, and reading. Trinh T. Minh-Ha, Bhanu Kapil, Lucille Clifton, Joan Didion, James Baldwin, Percival Everett, Jorge Luis Borges, Marcel Proust, and Kazuo Ishiguro are just a few of the greats in my opinion.

Fluent in French, Spanish and Lightroom, with working knowledge of Italian, Photoshop, Premiere Pro and kombucha brewing.
