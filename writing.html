MULTIMEDIA

"Savoirs des femmes" (digital humanities archive) - I translated, transcribed and digitized nearly 300 French postcard "faces" in a digital archive for a research project, "Savoirs de jeunes filles," on women’s roles in fin-de-siècle France. I curated the online project and co-presented it with Professor Virginie Pouzet-Duzer, who directed the project and collected the original artifacts, at the conference "The 19th Century in 2019: Mapping Women's Writing in the Long Nineteenth Century" (California State University Long Beach).

  • "Savoirs de jeunes filles" (chapter co-written with Virginie Duzer) - The Palgrave Handbook of Transnational Women’s Writing in the Long Nineteenth Century, forthcoming (2022).

"Howard Duck" (video remix): I remixed (Fair Use) the sleazy, short-lived 90s cartoon show Duckman with a self-identifying MGTOW (Men Gone Their Own Way, an anti-feminist and misogynist movement) Youtuber by the name of Howard Dare. The result is a satirical MGTOW recruitment video, using visuals from the former and audio from the ladder. Other source material comes from public domain PSA clips from the Prelinger Archives.

"Exotic & Foreign" (video remix): I remixed slam poetry performances by Summer Durant, Alyssa Harris and Angelica Maria Aguilera into a dialogue, with original source material from Button Poetry and Poetry Slam, Inc (Fair Use).

"La Regadera: jugando con visuales (Making of / Behind the scenes)" (video / open-source collab): As a contributor to AVFLOSS, a Madrid-based working group that does audiovisual projects using open-source software, I scripted and edited this behind-the-scenes / "Making of" video (using AVFLOSS members' footage) of La Regadera: jugando con visuales, an event that brought kids aged 0-6 together in a digitally mediated play space. This was my first time working with Blender, an open-source video editing and 3D computer graphics software. View it here on AVFLOSS' Peertube account or here on their Github site at the bottom of the page.
